Comparing the websites

08/10/2014

Tango MangoThis is best community, non-profit webiste out there for tango lovers. You can find lessons, practicas and milongas(fast style Tango) dances and save to your calendar. Not only it does serve tango lovers, but also it serves to tango teachers and organizers who would like to reach out to broader audience. The best part about this site is that it is all in one place , well organized, easy to use and very intuitive.Aside from that you can see all the events that you want to go in one page, after you sign in. I would like to do Native application of this websites as a final project during my DBC, if I can find boots that will help me out along the way.

CourseraThis is the early adopters of MOOC sites. Landing page of Coursera is very intuitive and user friendly. Especially, Saerch bar at the landing page is very practical and navigation bar makes it very easy to find to find what you are looking for.This website solves the college and masters level education problems by offering online classes from different universities for free. Web site is well-thought and organized. I always find myself happy while on this site.

MintedI love this site not only I used to work there but it is super user-friendly. Especially when you try to customize the product, it is very intuitive and engaging through friendly pop-up boxes. The brading is very well done not only on the website,but also the real product. All the categories on the produc landing pages are very informative, you can find the product easily by filtering throught the filters.


I will wrap up here and would like to hear your feedback through Github!
